The changes in the civil rights movement and the role of women had affected Toni Morrison significantly. The draft had called for “fifteen million men to answer the call to the war and the war increased the production of goods which means that it created a large demand of workers and women filled those jobs,” (Hendrickson). Before World War II most men had jobs and many women were housewives. During the war many men were enlisted to the military and there was more demand for goods and so women had to take over the jobs. In 1954 the Civil Rights Movement started, “the Civil Rights Movement is about giving rights back to the blacks,” (Hendrickson). Before the movement, the blacks were treated unfairly especially in the south and there were separations of the whites and blacks out of everything, the military, schools, and drinking fountains. The movement started with a “protest which started off with a bus boycott about not giving up their seats to whites and Martin Luther King Jr led the movement,” (Keese). The protest eventually grew over time, and it slowly changed the rights of blacks and the racism toward them.
